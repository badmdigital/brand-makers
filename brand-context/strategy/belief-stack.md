# Belief Stack — [Brand Name]

> Powered by Starts With Them methodology.
> The SWT Buyer Belief Ladder has 5 stages: Unaware → Problem Recognition → Cause Awareness → Solution Awareness → Provider Decision.
> A buyer cannot skip stages. Marketing fails when it pitches at a stage the buyer hasn't reached yet.

## The Decision

**Commitment Action:** [e.g. What is the buyer agreeing to do? Buy, book, enroll, subscribe, sign.]

## The Four Belief Layers

A buyer must hold all four beliefs before they're ready to decide. Missing even one kills the sale.

---

### Layer 1 — Situation Awareness
> The buyer must believe: "My situation is a real problem worth solving."

**What they need to believe:**
[e.g. Their situation is not normal, not inevitable, and not their fault — but it is urgent.]

**Buyer quote placeholder:**
> "[e.g. I've been dealing with this for a while but I always thought it was just part of the deal...]"

**Marketing rule:**
[e.g. Don't pitch the solution here. Name the situation. Validate the pain. Make them feel seen before you offer anything.]

---

### Layer 2 — Cause Awareness
> The buyer must believe: "I understand WHY this problem is happening."

**What they need to believe:**
[e.g. The real cause of their situation — and why their previous attempts failed. This reframes the problem in your favor.]

**Buyer quote placeholder:**
> "[e.g. I kept trying X but I didn't realize the real issue was Y...]"

**Marketing rule:**
[e.g. Teach, don't sell. Content that explains the root cause builds the most trust and pre-sells your approach before a pitch ever lands.]

---

### Layer 3 — Fix Awareness
> The buyer must believe: "This type of solution actually works for people like me."

**What they need to believe:**
[e.g. That a solution category exists — and that it's proven. Not necessarily [Brand Name] yet, just that the approach works.]

**Buyer quote placeholder:**
> "[e.g. I was skeptical, but when I saw how [someone like me] got [result], I thought maybe it could work...]"

**Marketing rule:**
[e.g. Use case studies and proof from people who match their profile. Abstracted results don't land — specificity does. Show the mechanism, not just the outcome.]

---

### Layer 4 — Provider Trust
> The buyer must believe: "[Brand Name] is the right choice to deliver this for me."

**What they need to believe:**
[e.g. That [Brand Name] specifically is the right fit — their situation, their budget, their timeline, their trust threshold.]

**Buyer quote placeholder:**
> "[e.g. I looked at a few options but [Brand Name] felt like they actually understood my situation...]"

**Marketing rule:**
[e.g. Proof, specificity, alignment. Show you understand them specifically — not just the category. Testimonials, credentials, and any friction-reduction (guarantee, trial, call) live here.]

---

## Belief Stress Test

Before launching a campaign, check all three:

- [ ] Does this message match the belief stage of the audience receiving it?
- [ ] Does every claim we make have a piece of proof behind it?
- [ ] Does this move the buyer one stage forward — not two or three?
